Group

A basic guide to verb groups in Japanese


Overview

Verbs are words that indicate action. In Japanese, verbs always come at the end of clauses and are thus usually essential to understanding meaning. There are three verb groups in Japanese, each of which have different rules in conjugation.

Group 1 (u-verbs, consonant-stem, 一段)

Group 1 verbs always end in a /u/ vowel sound. For verbs ending in /ru/, if the vowel sound preceding the /ru/ is an /a/, /u/, or /o/ sound, it will always be an u-verb.

Some examples:
  • 書く (kaku) - to wear
  • 使う (tsukau) - to use
  • 話す (hanasu) - to speak
  • 聞く (kiku) - to listen
  • 飲む (nomu) - to drink

    • Group 2 (ru-verbs, vowel-stem verbs, 五段)

    Group 2 verbs always end in /ru/ vowel sound. If the vowel sound preceding the /ru/ is /i/ or /e/, it is probably a Group 2 verb. There are certain exceptions to this rule, as some verbs ending in /iru/ or /eru/ are actually Group 1. However, all Group 2 verbs end in /iru/ or /eru/.

    Some examples:
  • 起きる (okiru) - to get up
  • 着る (kiru) - to wear
  • 見る (miru) - to see
  • 寝る (neru) - to sleep
  • 食べる (taberu) - to drink

    • Group 3 (irregular, 不規則)

    There are only two Group 3 verbs. Despite being the only "irregular" verbs, that does not mean that they are the only two that don't conjugate normally in to the other two groups.

    They are:
  • する (suru) - to do
  • 来る (kiru) - to come
