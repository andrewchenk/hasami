Existence

How to say "to be" in Japanese


Overview

The existence copulas/irregulars/constructs (ある, いる, and です, だ) are an interesting group of words in Japanese that all generally mean "to be". They aren't easily classified as a group of verbs, espeically when it comes to です and だ.

です/だ (desu/da) - is

です and だ are used to make an assertion of existence. For example:

なの です。

Nano desu.

It's Nano.

だ is a more informal term while です is more formal. While だ is often taught as the informal form of です in textbooks, the two aren't always interchangeable.

はかせ だ (ニャン)。

Hakase da (nyan).

It's the professor (meow).

ある (aru) - exists, inanimate

ある is used to denote existence for inanimate (not moving) objects. For example:

はこがある。

Hako ga aru.

The box exists/There is a box.

Take note of its unusual negative forms: instead of あらない, it simply conjugates immediately to ない. Depending on the situation, ある can be used to refer to things abstractly or objectively, even if they are animate.

いる (iru) - exists, animate

いる is used for animate (moving) objects. For example:

ねこがいる。

Neko ga iru.

The cat exists/There is a cat.
