Conjugations

How to conjugate verbs in Japanese


Overview

Conjugation is the creation of different forms of a verb. In Japanese, this largely consists of modifying the ending of the verb. Verbs will conjugate differently depending on what group they are in. Unless denoted otherwise, conjugations are derived from the plain form.

Stem -i

Though there are technically many verb stems, in Japanese verb stem usually refers to the masu-stem, that is, the stem of the polite conjugation.

We will use verb stem to refer to the masu-stem because it is not only used for polite forms but in many other conjugations and grammar patterns as well.

To get the stem:

  • Group 1: The last vowel sound changes from an /u/ vowel sound to an /i/ vowel sound.
    Example: 飲む→飲み
  • Group 2: Remove the る.
    Example: 食べる→食べ
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→し
    2. くる→き

te (Progressive)

The te form is a extremely useful form used for a variety of situations. It can be used to express a sequence of verbs, a continuative tense, requests, etc. Because of this, its function is not easily defined.

It usually ends with either て or で.

To get the te form:

  • Group 1: The conjugation will change depending on the last character of the verb.
    1. む、ぬ、ぶ→んで
    2. う、つ、る→って
    3. く→いて
    4. ぐ→いで
    5. す→して
    Example: 飲む→飲んで
  • Group 2: Remove the る and add て.
    Example: 食べる→食べて
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→して
    2. くる→きて
    3. 行く→行って*
    *Not really a Group 3 verb, but an important exception in this conjugation.

u (Plain)

The plain form is also known as the dictionary form or informal form. The plain form is used for casual situations, such as talking to inferiors are those who you are familiar with. It is also used for reference, hence the name dictionary form.

It usually ends with the vowel sound /u/. It is usually conjugated from, as the starting point of conjugation. However, it is useful to know how to conjugate in to plain form from the verb stem.

To get the plain form (from the stem):

  • Group 1: Shift the /i/ vowel sound to an /u/ vowel sound.
    Example: 飲み→飲む
  • Group 2: Add る.
    Example: 食べ→食べる
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. し→する
    2. き→くる

To get the negative plain form:

  • Group 1: Shift the /u/ vowel sound to an /a/ vowel sound and add ない.
    Example: 飲む→飲ま→飲まない
  • Group 2: Remove る and add ない.
    Example: 食べる→食べない
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→しない
    2. くる→こない

masu (Polite)

The polite form is used for formal situations, such as talking to superiors or people you don't know. It is also known as informal form or masu form.

It usually ends with ます.

To get the polite form (from the stem):

Add the stem to ます.
Example: 飲み→飲みます

To get the negative polite form (from the polite form):

Add the stem to ます.
Example: 飲み→飲みます

ta (Past)

The past form is used to denote situations in the past.

It usually ends with た or だ. It is best to conjugate the past form from the te form.

To get the past form (from the te form):

Shift the last /e/ vowel sound to an /a/ vowel sound.
Example:
  • 飲んで→飲んだ
  • 食べて→食べた
  • して→した

mashita (Polite Past)

The polite past form is used to denote situations in the past. It is more polite than the regular past form.

It usually ends with ました. It is best to conjugate the polite past form from the polite form.

To get the polite past form (from the polite form):

Remove the ます and add ました.
Example:
  • 飲みます→飲みました
  • 食べます→食べました
  • した→しました

ou (Volitional)

The volitional form is used for invitation. It translates to "Let's" or "Shall we".

It usually ends with the sound /ou/.

To get the volitional form:

  • Group 1: Shift the /u/ vowel sound to an /o/ vowel sound and add う.
    Example: 飲む→飲も→飲もう
  • Group 2: Remove る and add よう.
    Example: 食べる→食べよう
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→しよう
    2. くる→こよう

ba (Conditional)

The conditional form is used to express an "if" condition.

It usually ends with ば.

To get the conditional form:

  • Group 1: Shift the /u/ vowel sound to an /e/ vowel sound and add ば.
    Example: 飲む→飲め→飲めば
  • Group 2: Remove る and add れば.
    Example: 食べる→食べれば
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→すれば
    2. くる→くれば

eru (Potential)

The potential form is used to express the ability to do something. It translates to "can" or "able to".

It usually ends with /eru/.

To get the potential form:

  • Group 1: Shift the /u/ vowel sound to an /e/ vowel sound and add る.
    Example: 飲む→飲め→飲める
  • Group 2: Remove る and add られる.
    Example: 食べる→食べられる
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→できる
    2. くる→こられる

seru (Causative)

The causative form is used to indicate an action someone will make happen. Confusingly enough, it can also mean to let something happen.

It usually ends with せる.

To get the potential form:

  • Group 1: Shift the /u/ vowel sound to an /a/ vowel sound and add せる.
    Example: 飲む→飲ま→飲ませる
  • Group 2: Remove る and add させる.
    Example: 食べる→食べさせる
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→させる
    2. くる→こさせる

reru (Passive)

The passive form is used to act upon passive subjects.

It usually ends with れる.

To get the potential form:

  • Group 1: Shift the /u/ vowel sound to an /a/ vowel sound and add れる.
    Example: 飲む→飲ま→飲まれる
  • Group 2: Remove る and add られる.
    Example: 食べる→食べられる
  • Group 3: Memorize these.
    1. する→される
    2. くる→こられる
